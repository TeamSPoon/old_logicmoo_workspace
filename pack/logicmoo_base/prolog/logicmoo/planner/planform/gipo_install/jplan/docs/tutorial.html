GIPO Tutorial

This tutorial is divided into two main sections. If you are new to GIPO then we suggest that you work through the tutorial for non hierarchical planning first. In GIPO III the Life History Editor is the preferred method for creating domain definitions. The tutorial for hierarchical domains presuposes familiarity with flat domain model definition.  

Flat domains are distingushed from hierarchical domains on two criteria.
  1. State definitions in hierarchical domains may be defined relative to different levels in the sort tree. For example all physical_objects may be defined as being at a location but packages which are a sub sort of physical_object may be defined as being either in the state of being delivered or as waiting. For hierarchical models the full state for an object is defined in terms of the product of the states defined for the sort of object plus the states from the states defined further up the sort tree. In flat domains all states must be defined relative to a sort occuring at a leaf node of the sort tree.
  2. Heirarchical domains may have compound operators, called methods , which are composed from the primitive operators of the type defined in standard flat domains. Methods are little sub plan.
